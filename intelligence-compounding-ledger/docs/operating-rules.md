# Intelligence Compounding Ledger™ Operating Rules

These rules keep the ledger lightweight, useful, and safe to use.

## 1. Log Only Meaningful Signal

Do not log every AI answer.

Log only signals that change one of the following:

- a decision
- an action
- an asset
- a workflow
- an offer
- a risk control
- a system rule

## 2. Every Entry Needs a Route

Every logged signal must be routed to one of these destinations:

- Execute
- Evergreen
- Share
- Audit
- Offer
- Archive
- Discard
- Restrict

If no route is clear, use **Audit** or **Archive**.

## 3. Proof Before Promotion

Do not promote an insight into a public claim, offer, or operating doctrine without proof.

Acceptable proof may include:

- repeated use
- clear before/after result
- client or user feedback
- decision improvement
- revenue impact
- time saved
- risk reduced
- documented example

## 4. Keep Private Architecture Private

Public assets should expose the useful interface, not the private operating backend.

Use **Restrict** for anything that contains:

- private family or personal material
- client information
- sensitive financial data
- credentials or secrets
- protected internal system architecture
- legal, medical, or identity-sensitive information

## 5. Do Not Let the Ledger Become Clutter

A ledger entry must eventually be resolved.

Resolution options:

- Built
- Published
- Monetized
- Archived
- Discarded

## 6. Review Weekly

Once per week, review open entries and ask:

```text
What should be executed?
What should become an evergreen asset?
What needs proof?
What should be archived or discarded?
```

## 7. Compound the System

The final field, **Upgrade**, is the most important field.

Every strong signal should improve a system, rule, workflow, asset, or decision process.

If nothing improves, the signal has not compounded yet.

## Final Rule

The ledger exists to convert intelligence into action, evidence, assets, and system upgrades.

If it does not improve execution, simplify it.
